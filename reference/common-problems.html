Common Problems & Solutions

Every sewer runs into these problems. Here's how to diagnose and fix the most common issues.

Thread Breaking (Upper Thread)

Symptoms

The thread breaks frequently during sewing, requiring re-threading.

Common Causes

  • Needle not threaded correctly (missed a guide)
  • Tension too tight
  • Thread caught on something (loose thread spool, spool cap wrong size)
  • Needle has a burr or is damaged
  • Thread is old or poor quality
  • Going too fast for the material

Solutions

  • Re-thread the entire machine from scratch with presser foot UP
  • Lower tension by 1 number
  • Check that the thread spool turns freely
  • Replace the needle
  • Use quality thread
  • Slow down on thick seams

Skipped Stitches

Symptoms

Some stitches are missing from the seam line, leaving gaps.

Common Causes

  • Wrong needle type for the fabric (most common)
  • Needle is dull or damaged
  • Needle is not fully seated (not pushed all the way up into the clamp)
  • Thread not going through take-up lever correctly
  • Sewing too fast

Solutions

  • Replace needle with correct type for fabric (ballpoint for knits, sharp for wovens)
  • Push needle all the way up before tightening
  • Re-thread completely
  • Slow down
  • If sewing knits, use a ballpoint/stretch needle specifically

Puckered Seams

Symptoms

The fabric bunches or gathers along the seam line after sewing.

Common Causes

  • Tension too tight for the fabric weight
  • Stitch length too short for the fabric
  • Thread weight too heavy for delicate fabric
  • Fabric not supported — letting it hang off the table creates drag

Solutions

  • Reduce tension slightly
  • Lengthen stitch slightly (try 2.5–3mm)
  • Use thread that matches the fabric weight
  • Always support the fabric so it doesn't drag downward while you sew
  • Test on a scrap

Uneven Tension (Thread Loops on Top or Bottom)

Symptoms

Loops of thread visible on the top or bottom of the seam. If loops are on TOP → bobbin thread pulling up. If loops are on BOTTOM → upper thread pulling down.

Common Causes

  • Machine not threaded correctly (most common — usually missed a guide or the take-up lever)
  • Bobbin not inserted correctly or wound unevenly
  • Tension set incorrectly

Solutions

  • Re-thread EVERYTHING — upper thread and bobbin — from scratch, with presser foot UP
  • Check bobbin is wound evenly and inserted with thread going in the right direction
  • Adjust tension incrementally (one number at a time) and test on scrap

Needle Breaking

Symptoms

The needle breaks during sewing, sometimes with a loud snap.

Common Causes

  • Pulling or pushing fabric through the machine (fabric movement should come from the feed dogs only)
  • Sewing over a pin
  • Needle too fine for thick fabric
  • Needle not tightened fully in clamp
  • Using wrong needle for the job

Solutions

  • Let the feed dogs move the fabric — you guide, not push
  • Never sew over pins
  • Use a heavier needle for heavy fabric
  • Tighten needle clamp securely
  • Replace with correct needle type

Fabric Not Feeding (Fabric Not Moving)

Symptoms

The machine runs but the fabric stays in place; tiny loops of stitches pile on top of each other in one spot.

Common Causes

  • Feed dogs are lowered (some machines have a button to drop feed dogs for darning — accidentally activated)
  • Presser foot pressure too low
  • Beginning to sew from the very edge of the fabric with no fabric under presser foot

Solutions

  • Check if feed dogs are in the raised position (consult your machine manual)
  • Increase presser foot pressure if adjustable
  • Start sewing with at least 1/2 inch of fabric under the presser foot
  • Hold thread tails out of the way when starting

Thread Tangling / Bird's Nest Under the Fabric

Symptoms

At the start of sewing, a clump of tangled thread appears on the underside of the fabric.

Common Causes

  • Not holding thread tails at the start
  • Upper thread not threaded correctly through all guides
  • Presser foot down during threading
  • Starting to sew without lowering the needle into the fabric first
  • Bobbin thread not pulled up before starting

Solutions

  • Always hold both thread tails (upper and bobbin) to the back when you begin sewing
  • Re-thread with presser foot UP
  • Pull up the bobbin thread through the hole in the needle plate before starting
  • Lower the needle into the fabric before lowering the presser foot for your first stitch

Seams Not Straight

Symptoms

The stitched seam line curves or wanders instead of going in a straight line.

Common Causes

  • Looking at the needle instead of the seam guide
  • Not using the seam guide on the needle plate
  • Fabric shifting during sewing
  • Going too fast to maintain control

Solutions

  • Focus your eyes on the raw edge of the fabric and the seam guide mark — NOT the needle
  • Use a piece of painter's tape on the needle plate as a seam guide if the engraved lines are hard to see
  • Slow down
  • Pin more frequently to prevent fabric shifting
